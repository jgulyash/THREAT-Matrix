## What This Demonstrates

**Application of analytical tradecraft and operational depth to structured framework development**  
The tactic taxonomy was built using behavioral analysis methodology — function-first naming, actor-goal framing, phase conditionality — drawn from intelligence community standards for threat characterization. Every tactic name describes what an adversary is *doing*, not what the defender is *worried about*.

**Tactic-to-technique decomposition with sub-technique specificity**  
154 tactics across four matrices, each with behavioral descriptions, actor applicability, phase placement, and CPN annotation. The decomposition decisions — what merits its own tactic vs. what's a sub-behavior — reflect practitioner-level judgment about operational distinctiveness.

**Cross-domain thinking bridging physical threat intelligence, insider risk, and cyber threat frameworks**  
The Organization matrix treats insider threat as a trusted-access adversary behavioral pathway, not an HR policy problem. The Cyber-Physical Nexus (CPN) tagging and four-vector AI architecture reflect sustained analysis of where physical and cyber threat intelligence converge and diverge.

**Structured analytical methodology: behavior-first naming, actor-goal-conditional phase design**  
Phase 4's bifurcation (evasion-seeking vs. attribution-seeking actor tracks) is an example of the underlying methodology: design decisions based on behavioral logic, not convention.
